Medical Coding Expertise

01

CPT Codes

These codes are grouped into three categories. The first group is divided into numeric codes corresponding to different areas of medicine, such as radiology, surgery, & anesthesia. The second category contains alphanumeric codes identifying performance measurements & laboratory or radiology test results. Finally, the third category of codes is dedicated to emerging medical technology.

02

ICD Codes

The ICD codes are a system used to describe the cause of a patient's illness, injury, or death. The WHO established this code in the 1940s, & each update adds a numeral to indicate the code's version. ICD-10 refers to the 10th version of the code. Then, clinical changes are made to these codes, which make it possible to record a wider range of illnesses, injuries, & causes of death in detail.

03

HCPCS Codes

The HCPCS coding system is essential for recording medical services, equipment, or procedures that are not covered by CPT codes. It is the official coding system for Medicare & Medicaid, & it is crucial for medical coders to be familiar with it. HCPCS accurately records medical procedures, similar to CPT codes, to ensure patients receive the appropriate level of care.

Remote Patient Monitoring (RPM)

Real-Time Healthcare Through Technology

What is Remote Patient Monitoring?

Remote Patient Monitoring (RPM) is a modern healthcare solution that connects patients with their providers through technology, offering real-time insights into health and wellness. RPM goes beyond simply tracking vital signs—it empowers patients to actively manage their health.

This service uses advanced devices and digital tools to collect medical data from patients in their homes and securely transmit it to healthcare providers. Clinicians can then monitor health status, detect potential issues early, and make informed care decisions. RPM is particularly valuable for managing chronic conditions such as diabetes, heart disease, and asthma, helping improve outcomes and overall quality of life.
